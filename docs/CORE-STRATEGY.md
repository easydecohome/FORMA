# FORMA KITCHEN — CORE STRATEGY
*Output of the Core Strategy Prompt · July 2026 · Working doc, revise as reality reports back*

---

## 1 · BUSINESS MODEL MAP

### The one-sentence model
FORMA sells one modular kitchen system at two entry points: a fixed-spec **$3,490 standard kitchen** that buys customers, reviews and cash flow — and a **cut-to-order custom kitchen** ($6.5k–$12k AOV) that makes the actual money, online, Australia-wide.

### Product roles (do not blur these)

| | ENTRY — "FORMA ONE" | CORE — "FORMA CUSTOM" |
|---|---|---|
| Price | $3,490 fixed | From ~$760/lm → typical order $6,500–$9,500 |
| What it is | 1 standard layout (e.g. 2.4m + 1.8m L), 3 finishes, zero decisions beyond colour | Cut to the customer's walls, 3 collections, full estimator flow |
| Job | Cash flow · ad hook · review factory · trust builder | Profit · scale · the brand |
| Channel | FB Marketplace + site "Shop" page | Site only (quote funnel) |
| Margin profile | Thin (~30–40% gross) — priced to move | Rich (~45–55% gross) — priced on value vs $20k reno |
| Buying friction | Buy-now. No quote, no drawings | 48h quote → approval → deposit |

**The critical refinement:** FORMA ONE is not a product line — it is a *customer acquisition device that happens to break even or better*. Every decision about it (spec, price, listing copy) is judged by one metric: how cheaply it manufactures trust assets (reviews, install photos, video testimonials) and upgrade conversations ("can I get it 300mm wider?" → that's a Custom lead).

### Why this model wins vs the field
- Marketplace flippers can match $3,500; they cannot match cut-to-order + brand + reviews.
- Custom joiners can match quality; they cannot match price or a 60-second online estimate.
- Big flatpack players (Bunnings/Kaboodle, IKEA) own "modular standard sizes"; **nobody owns "cut to YOUR wall, online, at flatpack price" with a premium look.** That's the gap. The site's black-and-gold design is the moat around it — it looks $20k while charging $7k.

### Truth debt to clear before scaling (from the site audit)
1. Replace "in-house" manufacturing claims with "direct factory pricing" language.
2. Set a *defensible* lead time (overseas supply: promise the number you hit 9 times out of 10, likely 4–8 weeks — then beat it).
3. Real reviews/photos in place of placeholders — FORMA ONE's first 10 sales exist to produce these.

## 2 · GROWTH STRATEGY

### Highest-leverage path (in order)
1. **Weaponise Marketplace instead of leaving it.** It's free national demand. Every listing becomes a funnel mouth: sell FORMA ONE at $3,490, and every conversation/delivery pushes to the site ("custom sizes and 2 more finishes online"). Do not list Custom on Marketplace — scarcity trains the market that the brand is more than the listing.
2. **Harvest trust assets ritually.** Every entry sale: $75 handle-upgrade or rebate in exchange for (a) Google review, (b) 5 photos of the installed kitchen, (c) 30s phone video. Ten sales = the entire TRUST section of the site is real. This is the single highest-ROI activity in the first 60 days.
3. **Turn the estimator into a lead machine.** It already captures layout/metres/collection/estimate → Netlify. Add email follow-up sequence (quote day, +2d objection-handler, +7d "reviews from real builds", +14d offer). A quote that doesn't get chased is a lead you paid for and threw away.
4. **Paid social only after assets exist** (~day 30–45). UGC video + "Why are kitchens $20,000?" hooks, $30–50/day, retargeting first (site + estimator abandoners), prospecting second.
5. **Open the trade channel** (~day 60). Tiny-home builders, granny-flat installers, flippers — repeat buyers with zero per-order marketing cost. Direct synergy: Foldworks' wholesale audience and the Backyard BNB funnel both feed this. One landed trade account ≈ 5–15 kitchens/yr.

### Marketplace → Online brand transition (90 days, don't rip the bandaid)
- **Days 0–30 — Brand the listings.** Professional photos, FORMA branding in every image, "formakitchens.com.au" in copy. Insert card in every delivered pack: QR → review + $100-off-next referral code. Site gets the FORMA ONE product page (buy/enquire now, not quote).
- **Days 30–60 — Split the traffic.** Marketplace = entry only; site = full range. Start email sequences. Launch retargeting. Measure: % of revenue from site vs Marketplace (target: 30% site by day 60).
- **Days 60–90 — Flip the ratio.** Scale winning ads, launch trade page outreach (20 tiny-home/granny-flat builders DM'd/emailed with a trade pack), raise FORMA ONE to $3,690 on Marketplace while holding $3,490 on-site (trains buyers that the site is the better deal). Target: 60% of revenue from site by day 90.

### What NOT to do
- No showroom, no "we'll come and measure" as default (kills the model's economics — offer check-measure as a *paid* upsell, credited on order).
- No new SKU categories (laundries, wardrobes) until Custom does 15+ orders/month — same system can expand later; focus now.
- No discounting Custom. Value-add instead (free fittings pack, free delivery band). Discounts teach people the price was fake.

## 3 · PRICING LADDER & REVENUE EXPANSION PLAN

### The ladder

| Rung | Offer | Price | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 · Entry | FORMA ONE (fixed spec, 3 finishes) | $3,490 | Ad hook, cash flow, reviews |
| 2 · Core | FORMA CUSTOM (cut-to-order, 3 collections) | $6.5k–$12k typical | The business |
| 3 · Upsells | Benchtop package | +$1,200–$2,800 | Highest-margin attach; target 50% attach |
| | Fittings pack (organisers, bins, corner unit) | +$390–$690 | Easy yes at checkout; target 40% |
| | Handles/tapware curated packs | +$190–$450 | Zero logistics weight |
| | Check-measure service (credited on order) | $290 | Converts fence-sitters; pays for itself |
| | Install network referral | +$1,500–$2,500 | Kills the "I can't DIY" objection |
| 4 · Trade | Volume tier from 3 kitchens | −8–12% off Custom | Repeat revenue floor |

**Pricing psychology rules:** always anchor against the $20k renovation (never against Bunnings — punch up, not down). Quotes itemised so buyers can *cut* lines instead of asking for discounts. Charm-price entry ($3,490), round-number confidence on Custom quotes. Entry price can rise as reviews accumulate — test $3,690 at 25+ reviews.

### Revenue expansion model (targets, not forecasts — assumptions stated)
Assumes: solo operator + overseas supply working, ~$1.5–2k landed cost entry, 50% gross on Custom, ad spend starting day 40.

| Month | Entry sales | Custom orders | Trade | Est. revenue | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 (Marketplace) | 1–2 (organic/site) | — | ~$30k | Truth fixes, review harvest, FORMA ONE page live |
| 2 | 8 | 4 (retargeting + email) | first outreach | ~$60k | Ads on, estimator follow-up sequence |
| 3 | 10 | 7–8 | 1 account (2–3 units) | ~$100k | Scale ads, trade pack, raise entry price test |

KPIs that matter (weekly): quote requests, quote→deposit % (target 15–25%), AOV, attach rate on benchtops, review count, % revenue from site. Ignore everything else.

**Expansion after the 90 days** (sequence, not menu): (1) benchtop attach optimisation → (2) trade accounts to 5+ → (3) laundry/wardrobe SKUs on the same carcass system → (4) considered: financing partner (Afterpay-style on entry, project finance on Custom) — the single biggest conversion unlock for $7k+ online purchases once volume justifies the paperwork.
