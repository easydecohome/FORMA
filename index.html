Questions

Before you ask.

How hard is assembly, really?
If you can assemble flat-pack furniture, you can assemble FORMA — the difference is our panels arrive pre-drilled, edged and labelled in build order with a full-size plan. Most customers complete a kitchen in one to two weekends. You’ll need a drill, a level, a rubber mallet and a second pair of hands for wall cabinets.
What if a panel arrives damaged or doesn’t fit?
Photograph it and send it through — we remake and dispatch the replacement panel within 48 hours, free. Because every kitchen is cut from your signed drawings, “doesn’t fit” almost always means a transit issue, and transit damage is 100% on us.
How long from order to kitchen?
48 hours to your itemised quote, then 15 business days from approved drawings to dispatch. Delivery is 2–7 days depending on where you are in Australia. Most projects go from first email to finished kitchen inside six weeks.
What’s actually included in the per-metre price?
Carcasses (HMR E0 board), your chosen fronts, soft-close hinges and runners, adjustable legs, kickboards, panels edged on all six sides, and assembly documentation. Benchtops, sinks, taps, appliances, handles and delivery are itemised separately on your quote so you can see — and cut — every line.
Can I get it installed instead of DIY?
Yes. We maintain an install network in most metro areas — add installation at quote stage and we’ll coordinate it. You still save against custom joinery because the cabinetry itself is factory-cut, not built on site.
What does the 10-year warranty cover?
Cabinet carcasses, fronts and supplied hardware under normal domestic use — including hinge and runner mechanisms. It’s a manufacturing warranty, so it covers delamination, board failure and hardware failure; it doesn’t cover assembly mishaps, but replacement parts for those are cheap and we’ll talk you through any fix.
