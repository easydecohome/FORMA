The problem

You're not paying
for a kitchen.

Get three quotes for a small kitchen in Australia and you'll be told $15,000–$25,000. Here's the part nobody itemises for you: the cabinets and hardware in that quote are worth about $5,000.

The rest is the industry: a showroom lease, a salesperson's commission, a project manager, an installer's margin, and a markup on every one of them. An inefficient chain, and you're at the end of it holding the invoice.

01

The showroom you visit once

Prime retail rent, display kitchens nobody cooks in, staff on the floor. Amortised across every kitchen sold — including yours.

02

The hands it passes through

Factory → importer → distributor → retailer → installer. Everyone takes a margin, and none of them make your kitchen better.

03

The three-month wait

Quotes, revisions, scheduling, tradies in your house. You pay more and you wait longer.

The fix

We deleted the middle.
Not the kitchen.

03

One simplified system

Not 400 SKUs and a design consultation. One standard kitchen at one price, plus cut-to-order when your walls need it. Simple to build, simple to buy.

04

That's the trade, and we say it up front. A weekend with a drill and a level, instead of $10,000 for someone else's crew.

• Moisture-resistant board, edged on all sides.

• Stone-look benchtop, steel sink, cooktop — included, not "from".
